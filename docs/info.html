Incident Response Plan Portfolio

Introduction

The Incident Response Plan (IRP) Portfolio Project demonstrates my approach to handling cybersecurity incidents, outlining the methodology and steps involved in developing, executing, and refining a comprehensive plan. The objective is to ensure that organizations are prepared to effectively detect, respond, and recover from security incidents while minimizing business disruption.

Incident Response Phases

The IRP consists of several clearly defined phases: Preparation, Identification, Containment, Eradication, Recovery, and Lessons Learned.

Incident Response Team (IRT)

An effective IRP requires a dedicated team with defined roles including Incident Response Manager, Technical Leads, Legal Advisors, and Communication Leads.

Incident Detection and Reporting

Leveraging SIEM systems and endpoint protection tools, I ensure quick identification of incidents through continuous monitoring and efficient reporting systems.

Containment and Mitigation Strategies

Containment strategies are implemented immediately after detection, focusing on short-term containment and long-term fixes to secure affected systems.

Eradication and Recovery

After containment, the eradication phase focuses on removing the threat completely, followed by recovery where systems are restored to full functionality.

Post-Incident Review

After every incident, a detailed review is conducted to analyze the root cause, and lessons learned are documented to improve future response efforts.

Incident Response Testing and Drills

Regular drills and tabletop exercises are conducted to test the response plan and ensure that all team members are well-prepared for any security incidents.
