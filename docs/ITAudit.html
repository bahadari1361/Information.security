Context of the Organization

This phase involves understanding the internal and external factors affecting the organization, its stakeholders, and the scope of the ISMS.

Leadership

Leadership's commitment to the ISMS is critical. The senior management team defines the information security policy, objectives, and assigns resources.

Planning

This phase involves identifying risks, determining objectives, and creating a risk treatment plan. As a lead auditor, you ensure that the planning is comprehensive and aligned with ISO/IEC 27001 requirements.

Support

Ensure that the organization has the necessary resources, training, and awareness to maintain the ISMS.

Operation

This section focuses on the operational processes for implementing the ISMS. You ensure these processes align with the planned security controls.

Performance Evaluation

Monitor, measure, and evaluate the ISMS performance to ensure compliance. This includes auditing the internal processes and reviewing outcomes.

Improvement

Identify opportunities for improvement based on audit findings, and non-conformities, and implement corrective actions.

Internal Audit

Internal audits assess the compliance of the ISMS against ISO/IEC 27001 standards. You perform regular internal audits to ensure continued adherence.

Management Review

Management reviews ensure that the ISMS is effective, meets objectives, and supports the overall strategic goals of the organization.

Corrective Actions

Corrective actions are taken to address any issues identified during audits or performance evaluations. These actions help in continual improvement of the ISMS.
