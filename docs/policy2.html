ISO/IEC 27001 Information Security Policies

Central Bank of Afghanistan - Information Security Management System (ISMS)

Icon Policy Name Description ISO 27001 Reference
Information Security Policy (ISMS Policy) Establishes the organization’s commitment to information security, defines the security objectives, and provides the framework for the development, implementation, and review of security controls and procedures. Clauses 5.1, 5.2
Asset Management Policy Defines how assets, including information, systems, hardware, and software, should be classified, managed, and protected. It outlines responsibilities for asset owners and procedures for asset disposal. Clause 8.1
Access Control Policy Ensures the appropriate use of access privileges for users, systems, and networks, enforcing the principle of least privilege and need-to-know. It defines user authentication methods, account management, and access rights for each role. Clause 9.1.2, A.9.1.1 to A.9.2.6
Data Protection and Privacy Policy Establishes guidelines for managing personal data in compliance with applicable privacy regulations, such as GDPR or CCPA. It includes data collection, handling, processing, storage, retention, and destruction guidelines. Clauses 7.5, 8.2
Cryptographic Controls Policy Outlines the use of encryption technologies to ensure data confidentiality and integrity during storage and transmission. Defines policies for key management, encryption standards, and acceptable cryptographic algorithms. Clause 10.1.1, A.10.1
Incident Management and Response Policy Defines procedures for identifying, reporting, and responding to security incidents. It includes incident categorization, escalation processes, communication protocols, and incident investigation and documentation. Clause 16.1, A.16.1.1
Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Policy Ensures that the organization can maintain or quickly resume critical operations in the event of an information security incident or disaster. It covers backup procedures, business impact analysis (BIA), recovery objectives (RTO, RPO), and testing strategies. Clause 17, A.17.1
Physical and Environmental Security Policy Defines physical security measures to prevent unauthorized physical access, damage, and interference to the organization’s premises, data centers, and equipment. Includes environmental controls like fire suppression, temperature, and humidity control. Clause 11.1, A.11.1
