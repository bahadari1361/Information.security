Incident Response Plan (IRP)

Identification

ISO 27001 emphasizes the importance of identifying potential security incidents promptly. This includes monitoring systems, logs, and analyzing anomalies to detect unauthorized access or suspicious activity.

Containment

Containment strategies in ISO 27001 are aimed at limiting the scope of security incidents. This involves isolating affected systems or networks, preserving evidence, and preventing further damage.

Eradication

ISO 27001 recommends eliminating the root cause of incidents, such as malware or unauthorized access, ensuring that systems are completely cleansed before returning to service.

Recovery

Recovery procedures in ISO 27001 include restoring affected systems to normal operations, ensuring data integrity, and validating the security of systems before they are brought back online.

Lessons Learned

ISO 27001 stresses the need to document incidents and review responses to enhance future preparedness. This includes updating security policies, conducting root cause analysis, and improving incident handling procedures.

IRP Team

Incident Response Manager

Leads the incident response process, ensuring alignment with ISO 27001 standards and best practices, and oversees the coordination of all team members.

Security Analyst

Assesses the nature and scope of security incidents, performs detailed analysis, and follows ISO 27001 guidelines for effective incident resolution.

IT Specialist

Handles the technical aspects of containment, eradication, and recovery, ensuring that systems comply with ISO 27001's technical security controls.

Business Continuity Planner

Ensures that business continuity plans are integrated with incident response efforts and that recovery processes are aligned with ISO 27001's continuity management standards.

Communication Lead

Manages internal and external communications in accordance with ISO 27001, ensuring compliance with incident reporting requirements and maintaining transparency.
