Incident Response Plan (IRP)

1. Identification

ISO 27001 mandates that organizations promptly detect and identify any security incidents. Early detection is crucial for minimizing damage and reducing the impact on business operations.

  • Implement monitoring tools and alerts for suspicious activities (e.g., intrusion detection systems, log management solutions).
  • Regularly review system and network logs to detect anomalies or unauthorized activities.
  • Establish clear procedures for employees to report suspected security incidents.

2. Containment

The containment stage focuses on preventing the incident from escalating further while ensuring business continuity. This stage is aligned with ISO 27001's risk containment strategies.

  • Isolate affected systems to prevent the spread of the incident (e.g., disconnecting compromised systems from the network).
  • Identify key assets or critical infrastructure at risk and prioritize their protection.
  • Notify relevant stakeholders (management, security team, etc.) to ensure coordination of efforts.

3. Eradication

Eradication involves removing the root cause of the incident, such as malware, vulnerabilities, or unauthorized access, ensuring it does not recur in the future.

  • Perform a thorough investigation to identify the source of the incident (e.g., malware, external attack vectors).
  • Remove any malicious code or compromised files from affected systems.
  • Apply patches, security fixes, and reconfigure settings to prevent recurrence.

4. Recovery

Recovery focuses on restoring affected systems and services to full functionality. It ensures that systems are securely reinstated into the operational environment.

  • Restore systems from secure backups, ensuring data integrity and completeness.
  • Monitor systems closely after recovery to detect any residual issues or vulnerabilities.
  • Ensure that users and systems can resume normal operations with minimal downtime.
