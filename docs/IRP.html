Identification

Identifying potential incidents is critical. This involves monitoring systems and logs using tools like Nessus, Wireshark, and Splunk. In line with ISO 27001, proactive monitoring helps detect and escalate anomalies for timely resolution.

Containment

Containment limits the scope of an incident by isolating systems. This includes quarantining infected hosts, ensuring business continuity, and preserving forensic evidence for analysis and reporting.

Eradication

Eradication focuses on removing threats such as malware or compromised accounts. Steps include patching vulnerabilities and ensuring systems are clean before restoration.

Recovery

Recovery ensures the safe restoration of operations. This includes validating backups, reconfiguring systems, and verifying security measures in compliance with ISO 27001.

Lessons Learned

ISO 27001 emphasizes learning from incidents. This involves documenting actions, improving policies, and conducting audits to strengthen security posture.
