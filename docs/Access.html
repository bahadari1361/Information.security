Access Control Management

This system governs the rules and policies that determine who can access information and resources in an organization. It involves creating and managing user accounts, ensuring only authorized access.

User Authentication

User authentication ensures that only legitimate users can access the system by verifying their identity through credentials like passwords, biometric data, etc.

Identity Verification

Identity verification is the process of confirming the identity of a user based on predefined factors, such as username and password or biometric verification.

Password Policies

Password policies enforce rules for creating strong passwords, such as length, complexity, and expiration, to reduce the risk of unauthorized access.

Multi-Factor Authentication

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) adds an extra layer of security by requiring users to provide multiple forms of verification before accessing systems.

Access Granting

Access granting refers to the process of allowing users to access certain systems or resources based on their role and the level of authorization they possess.

Role-Based Access Control

Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) assigns permissions based on the userâ€™s role within the organization, ensuring users only access the resources necessary for their duties.

System Access Level

System access levels define what different roles within an organization are allowed to do in terms of system access, such as read, write, or administrative access.

Account Lockout Policy

Account lockout policies define actions taken after multiple failed login attempts, such as locking the account to prevent brute force attacks.

Intrusion Detection

Intrusion detection systems monitor access logs and behavior to identify and respond to unauthorized attempts to access systems or networks.
