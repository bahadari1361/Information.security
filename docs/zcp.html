Business Continuity Planning (BCP) Policy and Procedures

Purpose and Scope: The BCP aims to mitigate risks and ensure rapid recovery of business functions in the event of disruptions, including natural disasters, cyberattacks, and more. The scope includes critical business processes, infrastructure, and personnel responsibilities.

Governance: A dedicated Business Continuity Management Team is established to oversee policy implementation, training, and continual improvement processes.

Assessment Overview: Comprehensive risk assessments identify potential threats, vulnerabilities, and impacts on critical business operations. Identified risks are prioritized for mitigation strategies.

Business Impact Analysis (BIA): The BIA ensures understanding of dependencies and establishes critical recovery objectives, prioritizing functions essential for organizational survival.

Objectives: Recovery objectives are defined to limit downtime and prevent excessive data loss. These objectives are tailored to organizational needs to maintain stakeholder trust and operational integrity.

Communication Strategy: A structured internal and external communication plan ensures accurate information dissemination during crises to maintain trust and transparency.

Response Process: Incident identification, immediate mitigation, and activation of recovery protocols are detailed within the response framework to ensure swift and coordinated efforts.

Program Integrity: Regular drills, testing, and updates to the BCP ensure alignment with current operational and regulatory requirements.
