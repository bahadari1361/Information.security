Audit Process Details

  • Audit Planning: Define the scope, objectives, and timelines for the audit. This phase helps set clear expectations for the audit process.
  • Pre-Audit Documentation Review: Review of existing documentation, policies, and records, ensuring that all necessary documents are available before the fieldwork phase.
  • Fieldwork/On-Site Audit: Conduct interviews, document reviews, and observe practices on-site to assess the implementation of the ISO/IEC 27001 controls.
  • Risk Assessment & Control Evaluation: Evaluate risk management practices and the effectiveness of the controls in mitigating risks according to the ISO/IEC 27001 standard.
  • Non-Conformity Reporting: Identify any non-conformities with the ISO/IEC 27001 standard and report them with possible impacts on the organization's information security posture.
  • Audit Closing Meeting: Discuss findings with the organization's management and recommend corrective actions. This is a collaborative step to ensure that all findings are addressed.
  • Audit Report: Prepare a detailed report documenting findings, corrective actions, and recommendations for improvement based on the audit process.
  • Follow-Up Audit: Conduct a follow-up audit to reassess corrective actions and ensure that the non-conformities identified earlier have been adequately addressed.
