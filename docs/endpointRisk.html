Risk Assessment - ISO/IEC 27001

Central Bank of Afghanistan Project

Risk Identification

In this phase, all potential risks and vulnerabilities are identified. This includes:

  • Cyber threats such as malware and ransomware
  • Physical risks including theft and natural disasters
  • Operational risks like system failures and human errors
  • Regulatory risks impacting compliance

Regular threat modeling sessions were conducted to identify risks for both technical infrastructure and operations.

Risk Evaluation

Risks were evaluated based on:

  • Impact: The severity of disruption if the risk occurs
  • Likelihood: The probability of the risk occurring

A risk matrix was developed to determine high, medium, and low risks, ensuring prioritization for mitigation.

Risk Treatment and Mitigation

Strategies were implemented to reduce, transfer, or accept risks. Key activities included:

  • Deployment of firewalls, encryption, and access control
  • Design and testing of Disaster Recovery Plans
  • Regular employee training and awareness programs
  • Ensuring vendor compliance through third-party risk management

Risk Monitoring and Review

Continuous monitoring ensured that risk treatments remained effective. Activities included:

  • Regular audits and risk reviews
  • Incident tracking and feedback loops
  • Updating risk posture based on emerging threats
