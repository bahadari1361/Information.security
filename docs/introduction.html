Information Security Assessment - Introduction

Overview of the Central Bank of Afghanistan

The Central Bank of Afghanistan is the primary institution responsible for managing Afghanistan's monetary policy, overseeing financial institutions, and ensuring financial stability. As the regulatory body, it manages the country's national reserves, facilitates banking operations, and ensures the integrity of the financial sector. Due to the critical nature of its operations, the bank's information systems must be protected against a wide range of cyber threats.

Objective of Information Security Assessment

The information security assessment aims to:

  • Evaluate the existing security posture.
  • Identify vulnerabilities and risks to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of sensitive financial and operational data.
  • Assess the current implementation of ISO/IEC 27001, focusing on both technical and organizational security controls.
  • Provide actionable recommendations to improve security practices and achieve ISO/IEC 27001 certification, if applicable.
