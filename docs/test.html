Incident Response Plan - Identification

Identification Section – Incident Response Plan

The Identification phase of the Incident Response Plan (IRP) at the Central Bank of Afghanistan focuses on detecting and confirming potential security incidents. This phase is critical for timely and accurate response to minimize the impact of cybersecurity threats on the organization’s operations, sensitive data, and financial systems.

Objectives

  • Detect anomalies and suspicious activities in IT systems and networks.
  • Confirm whether an event qualifies as a security incident.
  • Categorize and prioritize incidents based on severity and impact.
  • Document the initial details of the incident for further analysis and resolution.

Key Steps in the Identification Process

  1. Monitoring and Detection:
    • Utilize automated monitoring tools such as SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) systems to identify potential threats in real time.
    • Integrate logs from critical systems like firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention systems (IDS/IPS), and endpoint detection solutions for centralized analysis.
  2. Incident Analysis:
    • Analyze alerts using predefined baselines and thresholds to distinguish between false positives and genuine incidents.
    • Collaborate with internal and external teams (e.g., SOC or CERT) to confirm the scope and nature of the incident.
  3. Classification and Prioritization:
    • Categorize incidents based on predefined classifications, such as malware attacks, unauthorized access, data breaches, or denial-of-service (DoS) attacks.
    • Assign priority levels (Critical, High, Medium, Low) based on the potential impact on the bank’s systems, data, and reputation.
  4. Notification and Reporting:
    • Notify stakeholders, including the Incident Response Team (IRT), IT administrators, and executive management, as per the escalation matrix.
    • Record all details, including date/time, affected systems, and initial findings, in an incident tracking system.

Incident Classification Table

Incident Type Description Example Indicators Priority

Insider Threat Malicious actions by internal personnel Unusual access patterns, data transfers Medium
