Context of the Organization

The organization must identify and assess internal and external factors that may affect the implementation of the ISMS. This ensures the ISMS is designed to meet organizational needs and security objectives.

Risk Assessment and Treatment

Risk assessment is the process of identifying security risks and determining the level of acceptable risk. Treatment involves selecting appropriate controls to mitigate identified risks.

Information Security Policy

This policy outlines the organization’s approach to managing information security. It provides direction for achieving objectives and securing information assets.

Asset Management

Asset management includes identifying, classifying, and managing organizational assets (data, hardware, software) to ensure they are protected.

Access Control

Access control involves ensuring that only authorized users can access information, systems, and networks based on their roles.

Cryptography

Cryptographic measures protect sensitive data both at rest and in transit, ensuring confidentiality and integrity.

Physical Security

Physical security involves measures to protect physical infrastructure and prevent unauthorized access to facilities where sensitive data is stored.

Operations Security

Operations security ensures the secure and efficient operation of systems and processes, preventing accidental or malicious information disclosure.

Supplier Relationships

Supplier relationships require that third-party providers adhere to the organization’s information security policies and standards.

Incident Management

Incident management involves detecting, reporting, and responding to security incidents to minimize damage and recover operations swiftly.

Business Continuity Management

Business continuity ensures that critical business functions can continue during and after a disruption, maintaining the availability of key information systems.

Compliance

Compliance ensures adherence to relevant legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements to maintain information security standards.

Internal Audit

Internal audits evaluate the effectiveness of the ISMS and identify opportunities for improvement in the security posture of the organization.

Management Review

Management review ensures that top management is involved in overseeing the ISMS and supports continuous improvement.
