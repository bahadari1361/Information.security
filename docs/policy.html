ISO 27001 Information Security Policy

Comprehensive Information Security Policies for Enterprise

Information Security Management System (ISMS)

The ISMS is a framework of policies and procedures that includes all legal, physical, and technical controls involved in an organization's information risk management processes. ISO 27001 provides the specifications for establishing, implementing, operating, monitoring, reviewing, maintaining, and improving an ISMS.

Access Control Policy

This policy ensures that access to information systems and data is controlled and only granted to authorized individuals. It enforces the principle of least privilege, ensuring users have only the minimum access necessary to perform their job roles. Regular reviews of access rights are conducted to maintain security.

Asset Management Policy

This policy defines the management of physical and information assets within the organization. It includes asset identification, classification, protection, and handling processes to ensure assets are protected against unauthorized access, misuse, damage, or loss.

Information Classification and Handling

Classifies the organization's information into categories (e.g., public, confidential, restricted) to ensure appropriate handling based on its sensitivity. This policy ensures that all sensitive data is adequately protected throughout its lifecycle, from creation to disposal.

Incident Response Policy

This policy outlines how the organization responds to and manages security incidents, including data breaches, cyberattacks, and other security events. It defines roles, responsibilities, and procedures for effectively responding to incidents and minimizing their impact.

Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

This policy outlines strategies for ensuring that the organization can continue operating during and after a disaster, such as natural disasters, technical failures, or cybersecurity incidents. It defines recovery strategies, including backup plans and emergency procedures.

Email Creation Policy

This policy outlines the process for the creation of corporate email accounts for employees. The policy ensures that all employees receive secure and unique email accounts based on their job role and department. Email creation is authorized only by IT administrators upon approval from HR and department heads. All email accounts are linked to the company's domain to maintain security and identity integrity.

Email Revoke Policy

This policy defines the process of revoking email access when an employee leaves the company, changes job roles, or breaches security protocols. The email account is to be deactivated immediately upon the employee's exit or within a specified time frame to ensure no unauthorized access occurs. The IT department handles this process securely to ensure data protection.

User Management Policy

This policy governs the creation, modification, and termination of user accounts within the organization's network and systems. It ensures that only authorized users have access to systems and applications based on the principle of least privilege. Regular audits and reviews of user access rights are conducted to maintain security and compliance.

Equipment Disposal Policy

The Equipment Disposal Policy outlines procedures for safely disposing of electronic equipment, including hard drives, computers, and other storage devices. All data must be wiped using secure methods to prevent data leakage or unauthorized access. This policy ensures that equipment is disposed of in an environmentally friendly manner in compliance with local regulations.
