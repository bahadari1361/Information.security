Key Components of an Information Security Policy

ISO 27001
Information Security Policy

Purpose and Scope

Clearly state the purpose of the policy. Define the scope of the ISMS - which assets, departments, or processes are covered.

Information Security Objectives

List measurable objectives aligned with business goals, including protecting information and ensuring business continuity.

Roles and Responsibilities

Define key ISMS roles, including IT personnel, security managers, and management responsibilities.

Commitment to Conformance

State the organisation's pledge to meet ISO 27001 requirements and continually improve ISMS compliance.

Risk Management Approach

Describe the organisation's process for identifying, assessing, and mitigating information security risks.

IS Controls and Framework

Summarise the controls, including access control, cryptography, and physical security measures.

DP Measures

Include provisions for protecting personal data and complying with data protection regulations.

Incident Management

Explain the process for detecting, responding to, and reporting information security incidents.

Training and Awareness

Emphasise the importance of regular awareness training for employees and stakeholders.

Internal Audit and Review

Outline the commitment to conducting regular audits to evaluate ISMS effectiveness.

Document Control

Outline the process for maintaining, controlling, and reviewing the policy to ensure relevance.

Consequences of Nonconformance

State the potential disciplinary actions for failure to adhere to the information security policy.
