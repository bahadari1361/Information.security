ISO/IEC 27001 Information Security Policy

Cybersecurity Standards for Protecting Information Assets

Purpose and Scope

This Information Security Policy establishes the guidelines for protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets in accordance with the requirements of ISO/IEC 27001:2013. The scope of this policy applies to all information systems, data, and physical assets associated with the organization's operations.

Information Security Objectives

Our key objectives for information security are as follows:

  • Ensure the protection of sensitive data from unauthorized access.
  • Maintain business continuity by minimizing the impact of security incidents.
  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements related to data protection.
  • Continually improve the information security management system (ISMS).

Roles and Responsibilities

The roles and responsibilities for information security within the organization include:

  • **Top Management**: Ensure the effectiveness of the ISMS and support continuous improvement.
  • **Information Security Manager**: Oversee the implementation and enforcement of security measures and policies.
  • **IT Personnel**: Implement security controls and ensure technical systems are secure.
  • **Employees**: Adhere to security policies, attend security training, and report security incidents.

Risk Management Approach

Our approach to risk management includes:

  • Conducting regular risk assessments to identify vulnerabilities.
  • Evaluating the potential impact of security threats on operations.
  • Implementing appropriate mitigation measures to manage and reduce risks.
  • Reviewing and updating risk management strategies based on emerging threats and organizational changes.

Information Security Controls

The following security controls are applied to safeguard information assets:

  • **Access Control**: Ensure that only authorized users have access to sensitive information.
  • **Cryptography**: Implement encryption to protect data in transit and at rest.
  • **Physical Security**: Restrict access to physical locations where critical information systems and data are stored.
  • **Supplier Relationships**: Ensure that third-party vendors and contractors follow security policies and procedures.

Incident Management

We have established a comprehensive incident management process, including:

  • Identification and classification of security incidents.
  • Response protocols for containment, eradication, and recovery.
  • Investigation and analysis to determine the root cause of incidents.
  • Documentation and reporting of incidents to ensure lessons are learned and prevention measures are taken.

Internal Audit and Review

Regular internal audits will be conducted to assess the effectiveness of the ISMS and ensure compliance with ISO/IEC 27001. Audit findings will be used to identify areas for improvement, with corrective actions taken promptly to address any nonconformities.

Document Control

The organization will ensure that all information security policies, procedures, and guidelines are maintained, reviewed, and updated regularly to remain effective. Proper version control will be applied to all security documentation.

Consequences of Nonconformance

Failure to comply with the Information Security Policy and procedures may result in disciplinary actions, including:

  • Formal warnings for employees who fail to adhere to security policies.
  • Termination of access to systems for individuals who violate security measures.
  • Legal action in cases of significant security breaches caused by negligence or misconduct.
