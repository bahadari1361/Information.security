ISO/IEC 27001 Information Security Policy

Cybersecurity Standards Based on My Experience as an Information Security Manager

Purpose and Scope

This Information Security Policy establishes the guidelines I followed while working as an Information Security Manager at the Central Bank of Afghanistan, ensuring the protection of sensitive data across all systems and processes. The scope of the policy covers all IT systems, employees, departments, and physical infrastructure that handle or store organizational data.

Information Security Objectives

During my tenure, I set measurable information security objectives aligned with organizational goals, including:

  • Protecting critical data from unauthorized access, ensuring confidentiality and integrity.
  • Enhancing business continuity by implementing disaster recovery and incident response strategies.
  • Achieving compliance with relevant legal and regulatory requirements.
  • Continuous improvement of the Information Security Management System (ISMS).

Roles and Responsibilities

As the Information Security Manager, my roles and responsibilities included:

  • Designing, implementing, and managing the ISMS in line with ISO 27001 standards.
  • Leading information security risk assessments, audits, and security testing (including penetration tests and vulnerability assessments using tools such as Kali Linux, Nessus, Nmap, and Wireshark).
  • Developing policies, incident response plans, and business continuity strategies.
  • Training staff on security awareness and ensuring adherence to security protocols.
  • Collaborating with IT teams to ensure systems are secure and compliant with internal and external regulations.

Risk Management Approach

My approach to risk management involved:

  • Conducting regular risk assessments using industry-standard tools like Nessus and Nmap to identify vulnerabilities and threats.
  • Evaluating the impact of potential threats on critical business operations.
  • Implementing risk mitigation measures, including technical controls (e.g., encryption, multi-factor authentication) and administrative controls (e.g., training, policies).
  • Performing regular reviews to ensure risk management strategies were effective and up to date.

Information Security Controls

The security controls I implemented to protect the organization's information assets included:

  • **Access Control**: Ensured that only authorized users had access to sensitive systems and data.
  • **Cryptography**: Enforced encryption for data storage and transmission to protect data confidentiality.
  • **Physical Security**: Restricting access to critical areas and data centers, employing surveillance and access control systems.
  • **Supplier Management**: Conducting security assessments for third-party vendors to ensure their compliance with our security standards.

Incident Management

My role involved establishing an incident management process to respond effectively to security incidents:

  • Defining incident classification and response procedures.
  • Implementing rapid detection and containment protocols for cybersecurity threats.
  • Coordinating with IT and management teams to investigate incidents and ensure timely resolution.
  • Ensuring compliance with reporting and documentation standards to meet regulatory requirements.

Internal Audit and Review

As part of continuous improvement, I led internal audits to assess the ISMS’s performance, identifying areas for improvement and ensuring compliance with ISO 27001 standards. Audits also helped in reviewing incident handling processes, risk assessments, and security controls.

Document Control

To ensure the effectiveness and relevance of the Information Security Policy, I implemented document control procedures. These included regular reviews and updates to policies and guidelines based on evolving cybersecurity threats and organizational changes.

Consequences of Nonconformance

Failure to adhere to information security policies resulted in disciplinary actions, ranging from formal warnings to termination of access to systems. The policy emphasized the importance of compliance to maintain organizational security and protect valuable assets.
